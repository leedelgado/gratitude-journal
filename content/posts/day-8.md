Today was focused on UX/UI and understanding how design impacts real user behavior and business outcomes. I learned that UX is not just about making things usable, but about keeping users engaged, building trust, and driving retention. Small design decisions can have a big impact on whether someone stays or leaves a product.

I also broke down key concepts like accessibility and visual hierarchy. Accessibility stood out to me because it’s about removing barriers and making sure everyone can use a product. Visual hierarchy helped me think more intentionally about how to guide users’ attention and make interfaces feel clear and easy to use.

Looking at real products helped me see the difference between something that looks good and something that actually works well. A product can have strong UI but still fail if the UX is confusing or overwhelming.

Overall, today reinforced that good design is about clarity, intention, and solving real problems for users.
