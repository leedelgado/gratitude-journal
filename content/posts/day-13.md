Today reinforced a big lesson for me: small mistakes can have big consequences if I don’t slow down and test properly. Looking at the CrowdStrike incident showed how something simple can break systems at a global level, and that made me realize I need to be more intentional with how I build and release things.

The debugging practice also reminded me that I can’t rely fully on AI. It’s a powerful tool, but I still need to think, test, and verify everything myself. The best approach is combining my own judgment with AI support, not replacing one with the other.

I also feel more confident about my final project. The feedback helped me focus on what really matters: strong questions and meaningful AI output. I’m starting to see that keeping things simple and intentional is the key to actually finishing something solid.

I’m grateful for the opportunity to learn these lessons now while building, not later when the stakes are higher.
