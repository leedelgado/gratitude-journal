date: "2026-04-07"

I’m grateful for the clarity I gained today around MVPs and how to actually build smarter. I learned that a strong MVP is not about building a full product, but about solving one real problem in the simplest way possible. Seeing examples like Dropbox and Zappos showed me that you can test ideas without overbuilding, while failed products like Juicero made it clear how easy it is to focus on the wrong things.

What really stuck with me is that simplicity is actually hard, and that the goal is to learn fast, not build perfect. I also started applying this to my own project and realized I need to focus on just one core feature instead of trying to build everything at once.

I’m grateful that I’m not just learning concepts, but actually building and starting to think like someone who creates real products.
