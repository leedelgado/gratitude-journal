Today was a big shift in how I think about building products. We focused on data, not just as something you collect, but as the foundation of how systems actually work. I learned how important data schemas are and how messy or inconsistent data can break everything. It made me realize that good structure is what allows you to actually get value from data.

We also talked about the business side of data and how companies use it to understand users, improve products, and make better decisions. That part really stood out to me because it connected directly to real-world impact. At the same time, we got into the legal and ethical side, and that was eye-opening. It made me think more carefully about ownership, privacy, and responsibility, especially with the kind of data I want to use in my own project.

What I appreciated most today was being able to apply everything directly to my own idea. I started with a few different concepts and ended up committing to the fitness selfie app. It felt good to take something from a rough idea to a clear, scoped MVP with a real plan behind it. I can see how the data flows through the product now, from input to output, and that clarity makes me more confident going into the build sessions.

I’m grateful for the progress I made today, especially in how I’m starting to think more like a builder and less like someone just completing assignments. I’m also grateful for the feedback from peers, which helped me stay realistic and focused. Overall, today gave me a much better understanding of how data, business, and responsibility all connect, and I feel more prepared to build something meaningful this week.
