---
title: "Day 9: Thinking Like a Product Designer"
date: "2026-04-19"
---

Today was actually a really good session. It made me realize that I’ve been jumping into tools too fast in the past instead of really thinking through the problem first.

Starting with paper forced me to slow down and focus on the actual user experience. Instead of worrying about how things look, I had to think about what I’m actually trying to solve. In my case, it was the real gym experience. Machines being taken, time being limited, needing to move fast. That’s something I deal with every day, so it made the design feel real, not just an exercise.

When I moved into Figma, everything felt easier because I already knew what I was building. I wasn’t guessing. I had direction. That was probably the biggest takeaway for me.

Another thing that stood out was accessibility. It’s not just some extra step at the end. It’s really just good design. Bigger buttons, clear labels, simple flows. All of that just makes things easier for everyone, including me when I’m in the middle of a workout and not trying to think too much.

The biggest mindset shift though was around scope. I don’t need to build a whole app. I just need to clearly solve one real problem. That’s it. That’s what actually makes something valuable.

Overall, today felt like a step toward thinking more like a product designer instead of just someone making screens.
