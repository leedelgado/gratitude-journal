I learned about root cause analysis and the 5 Whys, and it helped me understand that most problems are not what they seem on the surface. If you keep asking “why,” you can uncover the real issue instead of just fixing symptoms.

I also explored what makes a product good or bad. A big takeaway for me was how products fail when they don’t truly solve a real user problem. The Amazon Fire Phone stood out as an example. It had advanced features, but it didn’t deliver enough real value for users, which led to its failure.

This made me think differently about building products. It’s not about adding more features, it’s about solving the right problem in a simple and useful way.

I’m grateful for this shift in mindset because it will help me make better decisions, whether I’m designing, building, or evaluating ideas.
